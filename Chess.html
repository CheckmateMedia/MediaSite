How the game works:

There are two rows of 8 on each side of a chess board. Traditionally the pieces are white and black, or light and dark, and the person who has the white pieces goes first. Each player has 6 different pieces and 16 pieces total. The weakest piece is a pawn (8 of the 16) and can only move one space at a time and can only attack forward one space, diagonally.

The most important piece one has is called the king and if this piece is checkmated the game is over, thus our business's name. The strongest and most moveable piece is the queen. It can move forwards, backwards, left and right, any number of spaces as long as there are no pieces in the way. In the graphic on the welcome page that we created for this site, a ckeck mate is pictured that was achieved with a rook and the queen each turn you may only move one piece. Traditionally a move is final once a player lets go of the piece, so we teach students to think and plan as long as needed before finalizing a move.

A Brief History of Chess:
Chess was thought to be invented invemted in the 16th century in India, then spread to Persia, and then in the 15th Century chess moved to Europe and adapted into the version that we play today and the version that we teach here at Check Mate. In the 1800s chess tournaments began to be held. The first world chess championship was held in 1886. Then since the 1900s we have seen chess explode. The World Chess Federation (FIDE) was founded. Tournaments werre held to determine the best player in the world and then in 1997 a computer first beat a chess world champion.

Now let's dive into some of the greatest chess players of all time.
We will start with who many consider the greatest chess player of all time. His name was Bobby Fischer. He was the world chess champion from 1972-1975. Bobby was what we call a "grand master." At age 14 he became the youngest U.S. champion and at age 15 he became the youngest grand master. He was also one of the only American world chess champions. Sadly he died in 2008. Anatoly Karpov was a Russian grand master who held the title of world chess champion from 1975–1985 and 1993–1999. Another Russian master was Gary Kasperov. During the cold war, Russians were dominating in world chess cess championships. The current world chess champion who has held the title since 2013 is the Norwegian Magnus Carleson.
